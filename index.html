Getting Started

iKontroller transforms your iPhone/iPad into a controller for your Mac. Follow these steps to get started:

  1. Install both apps: Install "iKontroller" on your iPhone/iPad and "iKontroller Desktop" on your Mac
  2. Grant permissions: 1. a) Grant Accessibility permission on Mac: System Settings ‚Üí Privacy & Security ‚Üí Accessibility ‚Üí Enable iKontroller Desktop b) Grant Local Network permission on Mac: Settings ‚Üí Privacy and Security ‚Üí Local Network ‚Üí Enable iKontroller Desktop 2. Grant Local Network permission on iOS when prompted: Settings ‚Üí Privacy and Security ‚Üí Local Network ‚Üí Enable iKontroller
  3. Pair the devices First; ensure your iPhone/iPad and Mac are on the same Wi-Fi network, Cellular network or connected via USB-C/Lightning cable. Then on your iPhone/iPad; open up the pairing screen and click on your Mac's username. Accept this pairing request on your Mac and follow the on-screen pairing instructions and enter the 6-digit code as prompted. You are now successfully paired!
  4. Start controlling: You're ready to go!

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How do I grant Accessibility permissions on Mac?

A: Go to System Settings ‚Üí Privacy & Security ‚Üí Accessibility, then enable iKontroller in the list.

Q: My devices won't connect. What should I do?

A: Try these steps:

  1. Ensure both devices are on the same Wi-Fi network
  2. Restart both apps
  3. Check that your firewall isn't blocking the connection
  4. Try USB cable connection as an alternative

Q: The keyboard keys are "stuck" or not releasing

A: iKontroller has automatic key unsticking detection. If a key gets stuck:

  • Disconnect and reconnect your iOS device
  • Press the stuck key on your physical keyboard
  • Press or use the button/joystick that caused the stuck key(s) and it should get released
  • The app automatically detects and fixes double-presses

Q: Can I use multiple iOS devices with one Mac?

A: Yes! You can pair multiple iOS devices to a single Mac. Each device maintains its own custom layouts and mappings.

Q: How do I adjust touchpad sensitivity?

A: On Mac, go to Settings ‚Üí General and adjust the "Relative Sensitivity" slider.

Q: How do I adjust scrolling sensitivity?

A: On Mac, go to Settings ‚Üí General and adjust the "Scroll Sensitivity" slider.

Q: Does this work with games in full-screen mode?

A: Yes! iKontroller is designed to work seamlessly with full-screen/minimized/windowed apps and games on your Mac.

Q: What's the difference between relative and absolute touchpad modes?

A:

  • Relative mode: Used when gaming to move the in-game camera around (First person shooter, etc.). Moves cursor relative to current position, automatically recenters. The relative mode is depicted using a red eye on the top left corner of your touchpad.
  • Absolute mode: Used for direct cursor positioning aka moving your visual cursor around. Touch maps to exact screen location. The absolute mode is depicted using a blue cursor on the top left corner of your touchpad.

Q: How do I create custom layouts?

A: On iOS, tap the "Layouts" button, then "+" to create a new layout. Add controls from the palette and position/resize them as needed. Map the buttons and joysticks to keyboard keys

Q: Is my data being sent to any servers?

A: No. All communication is direct between your iOS device and Mac. We don't collect, store, or transmit any data to external servers. See our Privacy Policy for details.

Troubleshooting

Connection Issues

  • Devices not discovering each other: Check Wi-Fi settings, ensure Local Network permissions are granted on iOS and Mac
  • Connection drops frequently: Try switching to USB cable connection, or check for Wi-Fi issues/interference
  • High latency: Reduce distance between devices, close bandwidth-heavy apps, use 5GHz Wi-Fi, or switch to USB cable connection

Permission Issues

  • Mac Accessibility permission: System Settings ‚Üí Privacy & Security ‚Üí Accessibility ‚Üí Enable iKontroller
  • iOS Local Network permission: Settings ‚Üí Privacy and Security ‚Üí Local Network ‚Üí Enable iKontroller
  • Mac Local Network permission: Settings ‚Üí Privacy and Security ‚Üí Local Network ‚Üí Enable iKontroller

Mapping Issues

  • Mappings not saving: Ensure you've tapped "Done" or closed the mapping popup only after setting the keys
  • Buttons/Joysticks not responding: Allow the mapping version to get synchronized by disconnecting and reconnecting
