Setup Guide

  1. Download iKontroller from the iOS App Store
  2. Open the app and enter your username
  3. Grant Local Network permission when prompted
  4. Ensure your iPhone/iPad is on the same Wi-Fi as your Mac or that they are connected via USB-C/Lightning cable
  5. Wait for your Mac to appear in the device list or simply perform a manual refresh by tapping the "Refresh" symbol in the Device Pairing screen
  6. Tap your Mac to pair with it and follow the on-screen instructions

Creating Custom Layouts

Step 1: Create a New Layout

  1. Tap the Layouts button in the top-left
  2. Tap the + button to create a new layout
  3. Enter a name for your layout (e.g., "FPS Game", "Minecraft")

Step 2: Add Controls

  • Buttons: Add from the palette, resize, position, and tap to map to a key
  • Joysticks: Add to position, map each direction (up, down, left, right)
  • Touchpad: Add for mouse/cursor and in-game camera movement
  • Left and Right regions: Add for left and right single/double clicks and long press
  • Scroll Wheel: For vertical/horizontal scrolling

Step 3: Map Keys

  1. Enter Mapping Mode
  2. Tap a button or joystick; which allows you to map it
  3. Press any key on your Mac's keyboard
  4. Buttons are mapped to a single key and Joysticks are mapped to 4 keys(up, down, left, right)
  5. The key(s) will be captured and assigned to that specific button or joystick
Note: Custom layouts require iKontroller Gold subscription. The default layout is free and supports up to 10 controls.

Device Pairing

Pairing a New Mac

  1. Ensure your iPhone/iPad and Mac are on the same network or connected via USB-C/Lightning cable
  2. On your Mac, go to the Device Pairing tab
  3. On iOS, select your Mac from the discovered devices
  4. A 6-digit code will appear on your Mac
  5. Enter this code on your iOS device
  6. The devices are now paired!

Managing Paired Devices

  • Forget: Remove a device as paired and delete all its mappings and data
  • Block: Remove a device as paired, delete all its mappings and data and prevent the device from pairing again. A blocked device cannot send you any data
  • Unblock: Settings → Blocked Devices → Unblock

Connection Methods

Wi-Fi

  • Lowest latency when on 5GHz network
  • No cables required
  • Automatic reconnection

USB Cable

  • Zero wireless latency
  • Most stable connection
  • Requires Lightning or USB-C cable

Troubleshooting iOS Issues

I am not discovering my Mac on my iPhone/iPad

  • Check Local Network permission: Settings → Privacy and Security → Local Network → Enable iKontroller
  • Ensure both Mac and iPhone/iPad are on same Wi-Fi network or connected via USB-C/Lightning cable
  • Restart both apps
  • Check Wi-Fi settings (not using VPN or guest network)

Controls Not Responding

  • Ensure controls are mapped to keys
  • Check connection status indicator
  • Try disconnecting and reconnecting
  • Verify Mac Accessibility permissions are granted. On Mac; System Settings → Privacy & Security → Accessibility → Enable iKontroller

Haptic Feedback Not Working

  • Check Haptic Feedback toggle in settings
  • Adjust haptic intensity
  • Note: Some older iPhones/iPads have limited haptic capabilities
